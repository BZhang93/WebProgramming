Lab 4: OMFGDOGS

This lab is worth 5 points.

Lab Objectives

  • Practice writing HTML.
  • Practice writing CSS.
  • Start to understand the benefits of HTML5.

Overview

OMFGDOGS (http://www.omfgdogs.com/) was one of the best useless websites in 2012. It made a splash at ROFLCon in May 2012 at MIT. OMFGDOGS is now one of the great memes along with the Hampster Dance and Nyan Cat. One problem: OMFGDOGS uses Flash. It doesn't have to be.

Instructions

Using HTML5 and CSS, recreated OMFGDOGS without Flash by writing two files: index.html and omfgdogs.css. The animated GIF shall cover the entire body of the web browser and the tune shall play on page load.

Two assets are provided for you:

  1. The animated GIF omfgdogs.gif
  2. The tune omfgdogs.mp3

However, if the browser's width is less than 992 pixels wide, do not show the animated GIF covering the entire background! Instead, display the simple message with no styling necessary: No OMFGDOGS for you!. Audio can still play if browser's width is less than 992 pixels wide.

You must also put a link to the original OMFGDOGS website http://www.omfgdogs.com/ somewhere at the bottom-right of the page to give credit where credit is due. I urge you to use a bigger text size, different font, text colors other than black or blue.

Important Notes

  1. The tune must loop. That is, it is replayed when it is done playing.
  2. No CSS validation necessary.
  3. You are not allowed to use any JavaScript, including jQuery, for this lab.
